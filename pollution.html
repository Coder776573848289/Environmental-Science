Environmental Pollution

Definition

Environmental pollution refers to the introduction of contaminants that cause adverse changes in air, water, soil, or ecosystems, threatening human and ecological health.

Types, Causes & Effects

Type Causes Effects Control Measures
Air Vehicle emissions, industries 7M premature deaths annually (WHO, 2022) Renewable energy, emission norms
Water Untreated sewage, industrial waste 80% wastewater released untreated (UNESCO, 2021) Effluent treatment, Ganga Action Plan
Soil Fertilizers, pesticides, e-waste Land degradation, food toxicity Organic farming, bioremediation
Noise Urban traffic, industries Hearing loss, stress disorders Noise barriers, regulations

Nuclear Hazards & Human Health Risks

  • Chernobyl (1986): Affected ~3 million people.
  • Fukushima (2011): Ongoing decommissioning; ~1.25M tons treated water released (2023).
  • India: Concerns around Kudankulam & Jaitapur nuclear plants.

Solid Waste Management

  • India generates 62 million tonnes/year of solid waste (CPCB, 2021).
  • Only 30% processed — landfills overloaded.
  • Solutions: Segregation at source, waste-to-energy plants, recycling.

Case Studies

  • Ganga/Yamuna River: Despite Namami Gange, 80% sewage untreated.
  • Bhopal Gas Tragedy (1984): 3,000+ immediate deaths, ~15,000 long-term effects.
  • Delhi Air Pollution (2022): AQI > 400 during winter due to stubble burning.
  • COVID-19 Pandemic: Reduced CO₂ by 17% (April 2020) but increased biomedical waste.
